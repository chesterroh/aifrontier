Industry seems to have a growing influence on academia these days. At ICML 2026
in Seoul, the sponsor section took over an entire exhibition hall. Here are some
impressions from walking around, with photos.

## Big Tech

Companies like [OpenAI](https://openai.com/), [Google](https://about.google/), [Apple](https://www.apple.com/), [Meta](https://about.meta.com/), [Amazon](https://www.aboutamazon.com/), and [Microsoft](https://www.microsoft.com/) had the largest
footprints. They all ran things in a similar way:

- **Lightning talk sessions** — researchers took turns presenting their work on the booth stage
- **Researchers on site, by team** — you could walk up to a researcher in the area you care about and ask questions directly

Apparently Noam Brown also came by for a talk, but I couldn't make it — no time. 😢

Recruiting felt like the biggest goal.

A range of neocloud companies had booths — [Runpod](https://www.runpod.io/), [Together AI](https://www.together.ai/), [Nebius](https://nebius.com/), and more.

**[VESSL AI](https://vessl.ai/)** — a GPU cluster provider. A business where securing lots of good

<Figure

**[FriendliAI](https://friendli.ai/)** — inference optimization. The business model is leasing GPUs,
serving models on them, and selling the API — a game of how efficiently (price,
bandwidth, latency) you can serve.

<Figure
  src="/articles/icml-2026-sponsor-booths/friendliai-booth.jpg"
  alt="FriendliAI booth — The Frontier Inference Cloud for Agents"
  caption="The FriendliAI booth, advertising 2–5× faster output token speed and 50–90% lower inference cost."
/>

A side note: this company has deep ties to [vLLM](https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm), the open-source project
that first comes to mind for inference optimization. FriendliAI was founded by
[professor Byung-Gon Chun](https://bgchun.github.io/) of Seoul National University (currently on leave),
building on his lab, the [Software Platform Lab](https://spl.snu.ac.kr/). Continuous batching
(iteration-level scheduling) — now a standard technique in modern LLM serving
engines including vLLM — was first proposed in the team's [Orca paper (OSDI 2022)](https://www.usenix.org/conference/osdi22/presentation/yu)
(see the [FriendliAI blog](https://friendli.ai/blog/llm-iteration-batching)). [Gyeong-In Yu](https://gyeongin.github.io/), Orca's first author and
Chun's former PhD student, is now the CTO — and vLLM co-creator
[Woosuk Kwon](https://woosuk.me/) co-authored [Nimble (NeurIPS 2020)](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper/2020/hash/5f0ad4db43d8723d18169b2e4817a160-Abstract.html) in the same lab back when he
was an undergrad at SNU.

Korean companies like **[HyperAccel](https://hyperaccel.ai/)** (accelerators) and **[Nota AI](https://www.nota.ai/)** (optimization) were there as well.

## Other Booths That Left an Impression

**[General Intuition](https://www.generalintuition.com/)** — a world-model lab spun out of the game-clip
platform [Medal](https://medal.tv/). At the booth they were demoing **MIRA**, a multiplayer
world model trained on Rocket League.

<Figure
  src="/articles/icml-2026-sponsor-booths/general-intuition-booth.jpg"
  alt="MIRA multiplayer world model demo at the General Intuition booth"
  caption="The General Intuition booth, introducing MIRA, a multiplayer world model trained on Rocket League."
/>

MIRA is a "playable multiplayer world model" built with [Kyutai](https://kyutai.org/) in
collaboration with Epic Games. The [blog post](https://mira-wm.com/blog-post/), [technical report](https://mira-wm.com/paper), and
[code](https://github.com/mira-wm/mira) are all public, and you can even [play it in the browser](https://www.generalintuition.com/mira).
In short:

- A 5B-parameter diffusion transformer plus a 600M video representation codec,
  running diffusion forcing in latent space
- Trained on ~10,000 hours of Rocket League 2v2 matches collected via bot
  self-play — reproducing game dynamics like boosts and collisions purely
  through video prediction, with no physics engine or renderer
- Conditions on the key inputs of up to four players at once, running in real
  time at 20 fps on a single B200 GPU
- Despite training only on short clips, rollouts stay stable past the 5-minute
  measurement limit — in practice, for hours
- The training/inference code is open source, along with the 1,000-match-hour
  Rocket Science dataset (~4,000 hours across four viewpoints) [on Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/kyutai/rocket-science)

The funding story is a talking point too. They raised a $133.7M seed in October
2025 led by Khosla Ventures and General Catalyst ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/16/general-intuition-lands-134m-seed-to-teach-agents-spatial-reasoning-using-video-game-clips/)),
and eight months later, in June 2026, a $320M Series A at a $2.3B valuation with
participation from Jeff Bezos, Eric Schmidt, and others ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/general-intuitions-2-3b-bet-that-video-games-can-train-ai-agents-for-the-real-world/)).
Medal's action-labeled gameplay data (hundreds of millions of hours) is the core
asset, but as I heard at the booth, they don't sell the data — the plan is to
sell the agent models themselves.
